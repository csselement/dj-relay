# Private audio casting futures source notes

- Audience: product stakeholders.
- Delivery: portable HTML because a callable Data Analytics MCP artifact renderer and complete Sites publishing lifecycle were unavailable in this runtime.
- Decision: identify broad private-audio opportunities that materially benefit from an installed source appliance, then choose a near-term wedge without pretending qualitative ideation is measured demand.
- Required structure mapping: title; Executive Summary; evidence-backed market findings; ranked opportunity screen; recommended next steps; further questions; caveats and assumptions.
- Chart contract: categorical bar ranking nine concepts by a transparent founder-screen heuristic. Score range is 0-100; underlying dimensions are 1-5; bars start at zero. The screen weights recurring buyer and hardware leverage at 25% each, private-audio value and competitive whitespace at 20% each, and current AudioBox/Discus fit at 10%. This is judgment, not market sizing.
- Chart palette: single-root preferred; no redundant series or legend.
- Market evidence reviewed: Mixlr pricing/features, RØDECaster Pro II, Patreon Live, Transistor and Hello Audio private podcasting, Spotify podcast updates, ListenWIFI, Bluetooth SIG Auracast guidance, live translation services, industrial acoustic monitoring, Rainforest Connection Guardian, and AudioMoth.
- Quantitative market sizing was omitted because no authoritative cross-category demand dataset exists and fabricated TAM estimates would reduce decision quality.
- Product concepts, scores, and recommendations are hypotheses for customer discovery, not measured customer intent.
- Privacy, recording consent, accessibility compliance, music/sports rights, and AI translation accuracy require jurisdiction- and use-case-specific review.
